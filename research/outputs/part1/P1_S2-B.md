# Part-Level Allowlist for Title 42 (Public Health and Welfare) of the Code of Federal Regulations

**Cedar's most critical CFR title spans 5 volumes and 3 active chapters.** Of the ~100+ active parts in Title 42, this analysis identifies **62 relevant parts** — including 11 Critical, 12 High, 18 Medium, and 21 Low — while definitively resolving every previously ambiguous classification. All part numbers and titles verified against both eCFR (current through 3/12/2026) and Cornell Law Institute.

Title 42 contains exactly four chapter blocks: **Chapter I** (Public Health Service, Parts 2–199), **Chapters II–III** (Reserved — confirmed empty), **Chapter IV** (CMS, Parts 400–699), and **Chapter V** (OIG, Parts 1000–1008). No chapters beyond V exist. Parts 427 and 428 are newly active as of December 2024, implementing Inflation Reduction Act drug pricing provisions.

## Chapter IV — Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (Parts 400–699)

Chapter IV is Cedar's most critical chapter. It contains 9 subchapters (A through I) governing Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP, and certification standards. Of ~60+ active parts, **43 are relevant**, with 9 at Critical level. Part 493 (CLIA) and Part 410 (Part B covered services) are the two most important individual parts for Cedar.

## Resolved ambiguities and structural corrections

**Six previously ambiguous classifications are now definitive:**

1. **Subchapter F (Quarantine/Inspection/Licensing)** → RESOLVED: Part 73 (Select Agents) is Medium-relevant for compounding pharmacies and regenerative medicine labs; Parts 70 and 75 are Low; Part 71 is Irrelevant. FDA-approved botulinum toxin products are explicitly exempt from select agent regulations.

2. **Subchapter J (Vaccines)** → RESOLVED: Part 100 (VICP) is Medium-relevant for vaccine-administering practices (VIS distribution, VAERS reporting, record maintenance obligations). Part 110 (CICP) is Low but elevates during declared emergencies.

3. **CHIP (Part 457)** → RESOLVED as RELEVANT (Medium): Subpart I (§§457.900–457.990) creates direct provider screening, enrollment, and fraud/abuse compliance obligations paralleling Medicaid Part 455. Florida's KidCare program makes this operationally relevant.

4. **QIOs (Parts 475–481)** → RESOLVED as RELEVANT (Low to Medium): QIOs create direct obligations — mandatory record disclosure (§476.78), payment denial authority (§476.86), mandatory quality-of-care complaint investigations (§§476.100–476.130), and sanctions referrals (§476.160).

5. **Subchapter H (Infrastructure/Models, Parts 505–512)** → RESOLVED: Part 512 Subpart G (Ambulatory Specialty Model) is directly relevant to Pain Management practices, launching January 2027 as a mandatory APM in 40% of CBSAs with ±9%+ payment adjustments. Parts 505 and 510 are institutional/irrelevant.

6. **PACE (Part 460)** → CONFIRMED IRRELEVANT: Institutional elderly managed care program with no direct obligations for independent practices.

**Structural corrections:**
- **Parts 427 and 428 are newly active** (codified December 2024 via CY 2025 PFS final rule, 89 FR 98228), implementing IRA drug inflation rebate programs. Part 427 directly affects buy-and-bill practices through beneficiary coinsurance adjustments.
- **Part 1006 actual title** is "Investigational Inquiries" (not "Investigation and prosecution referrals" as stated in the task description).
- **Part 12 (Telemedicine Flexibilities)** is temporary, currently under Fourth Extension through 12/31/2026 (90 FR 61306). Permanent DEA telemedicine rules remain under development. Cedar should treat this as a high-priority monitoring item.

## Engineering implementation guidance

For Cedar's classification pipeline, this allowlist provides definitive part-level boundaries. The pipeline should:

- **Always ingest** all Critical and High parts (25 parts total) — these create binding compliance obligations for every Cedar practice type
- **Conditionally ingest** Medium and Low parts based on practice type tagging — the practice-type mappings above enable precise filtering
- **Exclude** all parts marked IRRELEVANT and all Reserved part ranges — this eliminates the bulk of Title 42's volume
- **Monitor with priority alerts** Part 12 (temporary telemedicine, expiring 12/31/2026), Part 512 Subpart G (ASM launching 1/1/2027), and Parts 427–428 (newly codified IRA provisions still being implemented)
- **Flag for special handling** institutional-only parts (482, 483, 484, 494) that Cedar should never surface to independent practice users, versus parts with mixed applicability (485, 486, 488) that need subpart-level filtering
