## Key Practice Considerations

### High-Impact Requirements
1. **Bloodborne Pathogens (§ 1910.1030)**: Universal requirement for all medical practices
2. **OSHA Recordkeeping (Part 1904)**: Injury/illness logs, sharps injury log, annual summary
3. **FLSA Compliance (Parts 541, 778, 785)**: Proper classification and overtime for non-exempt staff
4. **FMLA (Part 825)**: Required for practices with 50+ employees
5. **EEOC Requirements (Parts 1601-1636)**: Anti-discrimination policies, harassment prevention

### Medical Practice-Specific Sections
- **§ 1910.1030**: Bloodborne pathogen exposure control plan
- **§ 1910.1200**: Hazard communication for lab/compounding chemicals
- **§ 1910.134**: Respirator program if N95s required
- **§ 1910.151**: First aid and emergency eyewash stations
- **§ 1910.1020**: Employee access to exposure and medical records
- **§ 1904.8**: Sharps injury log requirement

### Documentation Requirements
- OSHA 300 logs retained for 5 years
- Employee exposure records retained for 30 years
- FMLA records retained for 3 years
- FLSA payroll records retained for 3 years
- EEO personnel records retained for 1 year
