# Cedar Session 2, Run 3: Part-level allowlists for CFR Titles 45, 29, 26, and 16

**All four titles have been mapped at the part level with every AMBIGUOUS classification resolved.** Title 45 yields **20 relevant parts** anchored by HIPAA (Parts 160–164) and the No Surprises Act (Part 149). Title 29 produces **~45 relevant parts** spanning wage/hour, OSHA, EEOC, and ERISA. Title 26 delivers **4 relevant parts** with healthcare-specific section ranges identified within each. Title 16 contributes **~33 relevant parts** led by FTC advertising, data security, and consumer protection rules. False negatives have been minimized throughout — when doubt existed, parts were included with LOW relevance tags rather than excluded.

## Deliverable 3: Title 26 — Internal Revenue

Title 26 is organized differently from other CFR titles — parts correspond to IRC sections, making Part 1 alone contain thousands of regulatory sections. The table below identifies relevant parts and, within Part 1, the specific healthcare-relevant section ranges.

**Subchapters B (Estate/Gift Taxes, Parts 20–29), E (Reserved, Parts 170–299), F (Procedure/Administration, Parts 300–499), G (Tax Conventions, Parts 500–599), and H (Internal Revenue Practice, Parts 600–899) are confirmed IRRELEVANT.** Subchapter B covers estate/gift/generation-skipping taxes — not medical practice operations. Subchapter E is entirely empty/reserved. Subchapter F contains general IRS procedural rules applying universally to all taxpayers, not healthcare-specific. Subchapter G covers international tax treaties. Subchapter H covers IRS internal operations.

### Key ambiguity resolutions

- **Title 45, Subchapter A (Parts 1–99):** Resolved. **7 parts relevant** (46, 60, 80, 84, 88, 92, 102) out of ~50 active parts. Part 92 (Section 1557) is CRITICAL. The remaining ~43 parts are HHS internal administration.
- **Title 26, Subchapter D (Parts 40–169):** Resolved as **PARTIALLY RELEVANT**. Part 54 is CRITICAL (contains COBRA, HSA comparability, ACA employer mandate, and full Chapter 100 group health plan requirements including No Surprises Act). Part 46 is MODERATE (PCORI fees). Medical device excise tax in Part 48 is **repealed** (December 2019).
- **Title 16, Subchapter E (Parts 500–503):** Resolved as **RELEVANT (MODERATE)** for practices selling supplements, skincare, nutraceuticals, or other consumer products requiring FPLA labeling.
- **Title 16, Subchapter F (Parts 600–698):** Resolved as **RELEVANT (MODERATE–HIGH)** for practices running credit checks, reporting patient debts, offering payment plans, or handling consumer financial data. Parts 660, 681, and 682 are HIGH.
- **Title 16, Subchapter J (Parts 910–999):** Resolved as **IRRELEVANT**. FTC Non-Compete Rule (Part 910) removed from CFR effective February 12, 2026. All parts now [Reserved].

### Critical regulatory status updates as of March 2026

- **Part 910 (FTC Non-Compete Rule):** Vacated by courts, removed from CFR effective February 12, 2026. FTC may still pursue case-by-case enforcement under Section 5.
- **Part 425 (Negative Option Rule):** 2024 expanded "click-to-cancel" amendments vacated by 8th Circuit (July 2025). Reverted to original 1973 text as of February 12, 2026. New ANPRM published March 13, 2026 to restart rulemaking.
- **Part 465 (Consumer Reviews Rule):** Active and enforceable since October 21, 2024. FTC already sending warning letters as of December 2025.
- **26 CFR §48.4191 (Medical Device Excise Tax):** Repealed December 2019. No longer a compliance concern.
- **29 CFR 1636 (PWFA):** Newest major EEOC regulation, effective June 2024. Requires accommodation for pregnancy-related limitations.
