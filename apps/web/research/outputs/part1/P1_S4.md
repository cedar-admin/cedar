# Cedar Classification Framework — Part 1, Session 4 of 8

# Domain Taxonomy: Level 1 and Level 2 Structure with Full Metadata

Based on the regulatory landscape analysis from Sessions 1 and 3, this taxonomy organizes ~20,000-29,000 relevant regulatory entities into practitioner-focused domains. The structure reflects how independent medical practices think about compliance, not how the government organizes regulations.

---

## Deliverable 1: Level 1 Domains

### 1. Controlled Substances & Prescribing

**Domain Code**: `controlled-substances`

**Description**: DEA registration, controlled substance prescribing rules, prescription drug monitoring programs, telemedicine prescribing restrictions, and Ryan Haight Act compliance. Covers all Schedule II-V medications including testosterone, ketamine, buprenorphine, and stimulants used in practice settings.

**Classification Signals**:
- **CFR**: 21 CFR Chapter II (DEA regulations, Parts 1300-1399)
- **Agencies**: DEA (high), SAMHSA (medium)
- **Keywords**: controlled substance, DEA registration, schedule II-V, PDMP, Ryan Haight Act, telemedicine prescribing, opioid prescribing
- **Statutes**: Controlled Substances Act (21 USC Ch. 13), Ryan Haight Act

**Cross-Classification Notes**: Heavy overlap with `telehealth` (telemedicine prescribing), `compounding` (controlled substance compounding), `state-regulations` (state PDMP requirements, state scheduling differences).

**Cross-Classification Notes**: Overlaps with `controlled-substances` (compounded controlled substances), `fda-regulation` (general drug rules), `state-regulations` (state pharmacy permits).
