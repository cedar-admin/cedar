### Title 16 Parts Needing AI Classification

   - **Why AI needed**: Pharmacy-specific advertising guide requiring nuanced understanding of drug pricing claims, insurance representations, and copay assistance programs
   - **Key considerations**: Interaction with drug pricing transparency, manufacturer coupons, insurance benefit design

2. **Part 243 - Guides for the Private Vocational and Distance Education Schools Industry**
   - **Why AI needed**: May apply to medical assistant training programs, phlebotomy schools, or other healthcare vocational training
   - **Key considerations**: Scope of "vocational school" vs professional healthcare education

3. **Part 254 - Guides for Private Vocational Schools**
   - **Why AI needed**: Similar to 243, requires determination of applicability to healthcare training programs
   - **Key considerations**: Distinction between vocational and professional medical education

4. **Part 260 - Guides for the Use of Environmental Marketing Claims**
   - **Why AI needed**: Increasing relevance to medical practices making "green" or sustainable healthcare claims
   - **Key considerations**: Medical waste reduction claims, sustainable practice marketing

### Classification Approach for AI

For each part requiring AI classification:

1. **Full text analysis needed** of:
   - Scope and applicability sections
   - Definitions of covered entities
   - Specific examples or use cases
   - Enforcement history in healthcare contexts

2. **Cross-reference analysis**:
   - Check FTC enforcement actions against healthcare entities
   - Review advisory opinions mentioning these parts
   - Analyze state AG actions under these provisions

3. **Industry-specific considerations**:
   - Part 241: Focus on pharmacy benefit managers, drug pricing
   - Parts 243/254: Check accreditation requirements for healthcare training
   - Part 260: Review medical industry sustainability standards
